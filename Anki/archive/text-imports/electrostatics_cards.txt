<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Coulomb's Law</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric force</span> between two point charges?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Coulomb's Law</b><br><br>\[F = k\dfrac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>F</b></td><td>electric force (N)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>k</b></td><td>8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>q₁, q₂</b></td><td>point charges (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>r</b></td><td>distance between charges (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Force falls off as 1/r²</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field — Point Charge</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric field</span> produced by a point charge?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field — Point Charge</b><br><br>\[E = k\dfrac{q}{r^2}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>E</b></td><td>electric field (N/C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>k</b></td><td>8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>q</b></td><td>source charge (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>r</b></td><td>distance from charge (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Field falls off as 1/r²</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Force in a Field</b><br><br>What is the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>force on a charge</span> placed inside an electric field?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Force in a Field</b><br><br>\[F = qE\]<br><br><span style='font-size: 14px; color: #555;'>Expanded (substituting E = kq/r²):</span><br>\[F = \dfrac{kq\, q_{source}}{r^2}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>F</b></td><td>electric force (N)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>q</b></td><td>charge in the field (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>E</b></td><td>electric field (N/C)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>This is Coulomb's Law approached from the field perspective</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Potential — Point Charge</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric potential (voltage)</span> at a distance r from a point charge?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Potential — Point Charge</b><br><br>\[V = k\dfrac{q}{r}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>V</b></td><td>electric potential (V)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>k</b></td><td>8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>q</b></td><td>source charge (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>r</b></td><td>distance from charge (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Potential falls off as 1/r — not 1/r²</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Potential Energy</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric potential energy</span> between two point charges?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Potential Energy</b><br><br>\[U = k\dfrac{q_1 q_2}{r}\]<br><br><span style='font-size: 14px; color: #555;'>Also expressible as:</span><br>\[U = qV\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>U</b></td><td>potential energy (J)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>q₁, q₂</b></td><td>point charges (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>r</b></td><td>distance between charges (m)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>V</b></td><td>electric potential at that point (V)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Falls off as 1/r — not 1/r²</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field &amp; Potential Relationship</b><br><br>How is the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric field</span> related to <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>electric potential</span>?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field &amp; Potential Relationship</b><br><br>\[E = -\dfrac{\Delta V}{\Delta r}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>E</b></td><td>electric field (N/C or V/m)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>ΔV</b></td><td>change in potential (V)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>Δr</b></td><td>change in distance (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>E points from high V to low V (hence the negative sign)</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field — Parallel Plates</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>uniform electric field</span> between two parallel plates?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Electric Field — Parallel Plates</b><br><br>\[E = \dfrac{V}{d}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>E</b></td><td>electric field (N/C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>V</b></td><td>voltage across the plates (V) — usually given</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>d</b></td><td>plate separation (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Field is uniform between the plates</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Capacitance — Parallel Plates</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>capacitance</span> of a parallel plate capacitor?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Capacitance — Parallel Plates</b><br><br>\[C = \dfrac{arepsilon_0 A}{d}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>C</b></td><td>capacitance (F)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>ε₀</b></td><td>8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m (permittivity of free space)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>A</b></td><td>plate area (m²)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>d</b></td><td>plate separation (m)</td></tr></table><br><span style='font-size: 13px; color: #888;'>Larger area or smaller separation → more capacitance</span></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Charge on a Capacitor</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>charge stored</span> on a capacitor?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Charge on a Capacitor</b><br><br>\[Q = CV\]<br><br><span style='font-size: 14px; color: #555;'>Expanded (substituting C = ε₀A/d):</span><br>\[Q = \dfrac{arepsilon_0 A}{d} \cdot V\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>Q</b></td><td>charge stored (C)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>C</b></td><td>capacitance (F)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>V</b></td><td>voltage across plates (V)</td></tr></table></div>
<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Energy Stored in a Capacitor</b><br><br>What is the equation for the <span style='color: #4a90d9;'>energy stored</span> in a capacitor?</div>	<div style='font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; text-align: center; padding: 20px;'><b>Energy Stored in a Capacitor</b><br><br>\[U = \dfrac{1}{2}CV^2\]<br><br><span style='font-size: 14px; color: #555;'>Equivalent forms:</span><br>\[U = \dfrac{1}{2}QV = \dfrac{Q^2}{2C}\]<br><br><table style='margin: auto; text-align: left; font-size: 15px;'><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>U</b></td><td>energy stored (J)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>C</b></td><td>capacitance (F)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>V</b></td><td>voltage across plates (V)</td></tr><tr><td style='padding: 4px 10px;'><b>Q</b></td><td>charge stored (C)</td></tr></table></div>
