# [CLUE] Mitochondrial Anatomy & ATP Synthase (compartments, F₀/F₁, drug-target logic)
> Cover everything below and lecture it from memory.
> (From Altius FLE 1 — Bio/Biochem miss, migrated from Anki 2026-07-05.)

- **Membranes & compartments**
  - **2 membranes → 3 compartments**: outer membrane | **IMS (intermembrane space)** | inner membrane / **matrix**
  - **cristae** = folds of the **inner membrane** — increase surface area, primary sites of energy production
    - Trap: **cristae ≠ a separate compartment**
  - **matrix** = innermost space — site of the **Krebs cycle** and **mtDNA**

- **ATP synthase location & flow**
  - sits in the **inner membrane**
  - protons flow **from the IMS → into the matrix** (down their gradient)
  - the newly made **ATP ends up in the matrix**, where it powers cellular work

- **F₀ and F₁**
  - **F₀** — spans the **inner membrane**; the **proton channel** (IMS → matrix)
    - Note: F₀ = pore through the membrane ("O" for the hole)
  - **F₁** — **projects into the matrix**; contains the **α and β subunits**; **site of ATP synthesis**
    - Note: F₁ = the factory in the matrix

- **Drug-target logic**
  - to hit a subunit, the drug must reach **that subunit's compartment**
  - e.g. a toxin blocking the **α subunit** (part of F₁, which projects into the matrix) → must reach the **matrix**
  - Rule: **locate the subunit → that's the compartment the drug must reach**
