## Peptide bond + four levels of structure

- **Peptide bond**
  - a **covalent bond** linking two amino acids (the **–CO–NH–** linkage)
  - forms via **condensation / dehydration synthesis**: the **–COOH** of one AA bonds the **–NH₂** of the next, **releasing H₂O**

- **Four levels — defined by / disrupted by**
  - **Primary** — the **AA sequence**
    - disrupted by: **any non-synonymous point mutation**
    - Note: a point mutation **guarantees** disruption of the **primary** level only
  - **Secondary** — **local H-bonds** between the **backbone carbonyl O** of one residue and the **amide (amino) H** of another (NOT side chains)
    - **α-helix** — H-bonds within a **single strand** (backbone coils, bonding to a residue further along the same chain)
    - **β-sheet** — H-bonds between **carbonyl O and amino H on different strands**
      - **parallel** β-sheet: paired residues are ~**2 residues apart**
      - **antiparallel** β-sheet: paired residues are ~**4 residues apart**
      - Note: the "different-strand" carbonyl-O↔amino-H wording = β-sheet (Q5 tell); same-strand = α-helix
    - disrupted by: must be **stated or implied** (e.g. denaturing conditions)
  - **Tertiary** — the **overall 3D fold of one chain**
    - disrupted by: must be **stated or implied**
    - side-chain interactions: H-bonding · hydrophobic · ionic · **disulfide bridges** (two cysteines → cystine, **covalent**, very strong)
  - **Quaternary** — **2+ chains assembled**
    - disrupted by: anything disrupting the **subunit interface**
    - Note: **dimer / trimer / tetramer = always quaternary** (oligomer = 2+)

## Digestion — proteases

- **Definition**
  - a **protease** (proteolytic enzyme) **cleaves peptide bonds** to break down proteins

- **Digestive proteases by location**
  - **Stomach → pepsin** (works at **pH ~2**, acidic)
  - **Small intestine → trypsin / chymotrypsin** (work at **pH ~8**, basic)
  - Note: acid stomach → acid-loving enzyme; basic intestine → base-loving enzymes

- **Not proteases (don't get baited)**
  - **lipases** = fats · **amylases** = carbs

## Folding assay — intrinsic tryptophan fluorescence

- **The physics**
  - Trp's **indole ring absorbs UV (~280 nm)** and **emits at a longer wavelength**
  - emission depends on the **local environment**:
    - **buried in hydrophobic core** → **brighter, blue-shifted**
    - **solvent-exposed** → **quenched, red-shifted**

- **As a folding readout**
  - as a protein folds → Trp **buries** → **fluorescence intensity rises over time**
  - fit that rise to a **first-order rate** → gives the **folding rate constant**

- **What it does NOT do**
  - it is a **passive readout** — does **NOT** give **secondary structure**, and does **NOT** act as a **chaperone**
  - Trap (Q44): "provides secondary structure" is **wrong** (that's CD spectroscopy, not Trp fluorescence)
