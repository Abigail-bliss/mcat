# [CLUE] Protein Purification (match method to property)
> Cover everything below and lecture it from memory.
> (From Altius FLE 1 Q11 — Orgo/biochem-technique weakness, missed.)

- **Core idea:** pick the separation method by the **property** that differs between your protein and the junk.

- **By SIZE → Size-exclusion (gel-filtration) chromatography**
  - beads with pores; **large molecules elute FIRST** (can't enter pores, take the short path)
  - small molecules get trapped in pores → elute later
  - Note: counterintuitive — big = fast out

- **By CHARGE → Ion-exchange chromatography**
  - **anion-exchange** column is positively charged → binds **negatively** charged proteins
  - **cation-exchange** column is negatively charged → binds **positively** charged proteins
  - elute by changing pH or salt (out-competes the binding)
  - Note: name = what the column *grabs* (anion-exchange grabs anions)

- **By SPECIFIC TAG / ligand → Affinity chromatography**
  - column carries a ligand your protein specifically binds
  - **His-tag binds Ni²⁺** resin → one-step, very pure
  - Note: the most selective method — one step if the tag is unique

- **By HYDROPHOBICITY → Hydrophobic interaction chromatography (HIC)**
  - **bind at HIGH salt** (salt strengthens hydrophobic interactions between protein and column)
  - **elute at LOW salt** (removing salt weakens hydrophobic interactions → protein releases)
  - Note: Q11 CPF trap — "increasing salt = elutes" is WRONG. High salt = sticks. Low salt = elutes.
  - Note: this is the reverse of the intuitive feel — contrast with ion-exchange, where salt also competes but in the opposite sense. With HIC, just memorize: **salt on = bind, salt off = elute**.
