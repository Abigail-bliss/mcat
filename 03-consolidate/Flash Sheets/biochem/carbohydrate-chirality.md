# [CLUE] Carbohydrate Chirality — D vs. L, natural sugars
> Cover everything below and lecture it from memory.

- **All naturally occurring sugars are chiral**
  - They exist as the **D-enantiomer** in nature (not L)
  - L-sugars exist chemically but are not found in living systems
  - Note: same logic as amino acids — nature picks one handedness (D-sugars, L-amino acids)

- **How to determine D vs. L — the reference carbon rule**
  - Find the **highest-numbered chiral carbon** (the one farthest from the carbonyl group)
  - In a **Fischer projection**:
    - –OH on the **right** → **D-sugar**
    - –OH on the **left** → **L-sugar**
  - Note: D/L describes absolute configuration at one carbon only — it does NOT mean dextrorotatory/levorotatory (+/−)

- **Common naturally occurring D-monosaccharides**
  - **Aldoses**: D-glucose, D-galactose, D-mannose, D-ribose, D-deoxyribose
  - **Ketoses**: D-fructose
  - Note: all six-carbon sugars on the MCAT are D unless stated otherwise

- **Chiral centers in hexoses**
  - Glucose has **4 chiral centers** → 2⁴ = 16 possible stereoisomers (8 D/L pairs)
  - Each stereoisomer is a different sugar (e.g. glucose, galactose, mannose are all C₆H₁₂O₆ but different stereoisomers)
