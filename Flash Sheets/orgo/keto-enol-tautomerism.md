# [CLUE] Keto-Enol Tautomerism (the alpha-H shift, and which tautomer wins)
> Cover everything below and lecture it from memory.
> (Self-taught 2026-07-01 — Orgo = #1 weakness. Building fluency, not from a miss.)

- **Core idea:** tautomers are **constitutional isomers that interconvert by moving one H + one double bond**. Keto ⇌ enol is the classic pair.
  - **Keto** form = the carbonyl: **C=O** with an **alpha carbon** next to it bearing an **alpha H**.
  - **Enol** form = **C=C** double bond + an **–OH** (the name spells the structure: **en**e + **ol** = alkene + alcohol).
  - Note: it's a real bond reorganization, not resonance — the atoms move (an H physically relocates). Resonance only moves electrons.

- **The vocabulary that makes or breaks it** (get this exactly right)
  - **Carbonyl carbon** = the C double-bonded to O.
  - **Alpha (α) carbon** = the carbon **directly adjacent** to the carbonyl carbon.
  - **Alpha hydrogen** = an H on that alpha carbon — **this is the H that moves.**
  - Note: the moving H is the **alpha H**, NOT an H two carbons away. If a carbonyl has **no alpha H**, it **cannot tautomerize** (e.g. formaldehyde-type / no α-H → no enol).

- **The mechanism, keto → enol** (three moves)
  1. An **alpha H** leaves the alpha carbon.
  2. That H lands on the **oxygen** → C=O becomes C–**OH**.
  3. The double bond shifts **from C=O to between the carbonyl C and the alpha C** (now C=C).
  - Note: the double bond doesn't wander freely — it goes specifically to the **carbonyl-C–alpha-C** bond. "More stable position" matters only when there's a *choice* of alpha carbon (unsymmetrical ketone).

- **Which tautomer dominates?** — usually **KETO**
  - Simple aldehydes/ketones sit almost entirely (>99%) in the keto form at equilibrium (C=O is strong).
  - Note: MCAT rarely tests the rule; it tests the **exceptions** below.

- **The two exceptions where ENOL wins (the traps)**
  - **Phenol** — the enol form is **aromatic**, so it's locked in the enol; it does NOT revert to a keto (cyclohexadienone) form. Aromaticity > carbonyl stability.
  - **1,3-dicarbonyls** (e.g. acetylacetone, β-ketoesters) — the enol is stabilized by **conjugation** with the second carbonyl **plus an intramolecular H-bond** (the OH H-bonds to the other C=O). Significant enol content.
  - Note: mnemonic — enol wins when it buys **aromaticity** (phenol) or **conjugation + H-bond** (1,3-dicarbonyl). Otherwise keto.

- **Why it matters (payoff):** enols/enolates are the **nucleophilic alpha carbon** behind alpha-halogenation, aldol reactions, and alpha-substitution. Acid or base catalyzes the tautomerization; the enol/enolate is the reactive intermediate that attacks electrophiles.
