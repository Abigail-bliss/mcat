# Biological and Biochemical Foundations of Living Systems (BB) Section

## Passage 6 (Questions 32–36)

The topoisomerase class of enzymes stabilize DNA topology by reducing superhelical tension and preventing knot
formation. Eukaryotic topoisomerase enzymes accomplish this by introducing a transient single-strand break in one strand
of duplex DNA. Human DNA Topoisomerase Ib (topoIb) is a 765-amino acid monomeric enzyme composed of four domains: the
N-terminal domain, which is highly charged and responsible for several protein-protein interactions, the core domain,
which embraces the DNA during catalysis, the linker domain, and the C-terminal domain, which contains the catalytic
tyrosine active site. TopoIb promotes the relaxation of supercoiled DNA by nicking, and later rejoining, one of the
strands of the DNA in a controlled rotary mechanism. This process results in the formation of a transient phosphotyrosyl
bond.

Under certain conditions, topoIb becomes trapped on the DNA, ultimately leading to cell death. Camptothecin is a natural
plant product with antitumor activity. Camptothecin stabilizes the covalent topoIb-DNA intermediate, trapping the enzyme
on the DNA. Camptothecin is a noncompetitive topoIb inhibitor, binding only to the covalent intermediate. Several widely
used chemotherapeutic agents have been developed to mimic the mechanism of action of camptothecin.

### Question 33 ✓ (correct)

Which of the Lineweaver-Burk plots shown depicts the inhibition of topoIb by camptothecin?

- _Subtopic 76 — Kinetics (general, Michaelis-Menten, cooperativity) · Topic 22 — Control of Enzyme Activity · SIR 4:
  Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 1: Biochemistry_

> _Four Lineweaver-Burk (double-reciprocal) plots:_
> - _**A** — axes 1/V (y) vs. 1/[S] (x); "inhibitor" and "no inhibitor" lines share the **same y-intercept** (cross on
>   the y-axis) → competitive._
> - _**B** — axes 1/[S] (y) vs. 1/V (x) — **axes swapped/mislabeled** (V not plotted against [S])._
> - _**C** — axes 1/V (y) vs. 1/[S] (x); inhibitor and no-inhibitor lines share the **same x-intercept** (cross on the
>   x-axis), different slopes & y-intercepts → noncompetitive. **✓**_
> - _**D** — axes 1/[S] (y) vs. 1/V (x) — **axes swapped/mislabeled.**_

- A. (competitive plot — same y-intercept)
- B. (axes swapped)
- **C. (noncompetitive plot — same x-intercept) ✓ (correct)**
- D. (axes swapped)

**Solution:** Choice C is the correct answer. The Lineweaver-Burk plot, or double reciprocal plot, allows for analysis of
reaction velocity as a function of substrate concentration. Based on the labeled axes, neither B nor D can be the correct
answer because the double reciprocal plot analyzed reaction Velocity (_V_) as a function of Substrate concentration
([_S_]). The independent variable of Substrate concentration must be shown on the x-axis in order to determine the method
of inhibition using a Lineweaver-Burk plot. In a Lineweaver-Burk plot competitive inhibitors have the same y-intercept as
uninhibited enzyme, but there are different slopes and x-intercepts between the two data sets. Non-competitive inhibition
produces plots with the same x-intercept as uninhibited enzyme but different slopes and y-intercepts. Uncompetitive
inhibition causes different intercepts on both the y- and x-axes. Answer A is a plot of competitive inhibition, which is
not the mechanism of camptothecin. Answer C is the correct choice, showing both inhibited and uninhibited enzyme
reactions with the same x-intercept, as expected for a non-competitive inhibitor such as camptothecin.

### Question 34 ✗ (missed)

If the average molecular weight of the proteinogenic amino acids is 110 Da each, what is the approximate molecular weight
of topoIb?

- _Topic 52 — Amino Acids · SIR 4: Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 1: Biochemistry_

- A. 76.5 Da
- B. 76.5 kDa _(your answer)_
- **C. 84 kDa ✓ (correct)**
- D. 340 kDa

**Solution:** Answer C is correct. The average amino acid is 110 Da, and topoIb is described as a monomeric enzyme. The
answer is calculated using the average molecular weight of an amino acid = 110 Da, and the number of amino acids in
topoIb given in the passage: 765 × 110 = 84,150 Da, or 84 kDa, so choice C is the best answer. Answers A confuses kDa
with Da. As written, it suggests that the entire topoIb molecule weighs less than the average weight of a single amino
acid. Answer B multiplies by 100 Da instead of 110 Da. Answer D is too large by nearly a factor of four.

### Question 35 ✗ (missed)

The topoIb catalytic cycle cleaves a covalent bond between phosphorous and which other atom?

- _Subtopic 1105 — General · Topic 186 — Acid Derivatives (Anhydrides, Amides, Esters) · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and
  Problem Solving · Discipline 5: Organic Chemistry_

- A. Nitrogen
- B. Carbon
- C. Sulfur _(your answer)_
- **D. Oxygen ✓ (correct)**

**Solution:** Choice D is correct. Recall that DNA has a double helix structure. The outer edges are formed by
alternating deoxyribose sugar molecules and phosphate groups, which make up the sugar-phosphate (or phosphodiester)
backbone. A phosphate group is a phosphorus atom bound to four oxygen atoms. The phosphodiester bond is the linkage
between the 3' carbon atom of one sugar molecule and the 5' carbon atom of another. The covalent phosphotyrosyl
intermediate between any enzyme and DNA requires that the phosphodiester backbone of the DNA must be the target of
cleavage. While all four options are atoms found in DNA, oxygen is the only choice found in a phosphate group. D is
therefore the correct answer.

### Question 36 ✗ (missed)

Replacing the topoIb catalytic tyrosine with which amino acid best mimics wild-type topoIb, while eliminating catalysis?

- _Topic 52 — Amino Acids · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 1: Biochemistry_

- **A. Phenylalanine ✓ (correct)**
- B. Threonine _(your answer)_
- C. Tryptophan
- D. Alanine

**Solution:** Choice A is the best answer. Recall that Tyrosine is a polar aromatic amino acid that contains a hydroxyl
group attached to an aromatic ring. Phenylalanine and tryptophan are the only options that include a bulky ring structure
similar to the tyrosine side chain, meaning neither threonine nor alanine provide adequate mass or hydrophobicity to
mimic wild-type topoIb. Thus, neither B nor D can be the correct answer. Tryptophan, however, carries two rings, making
the side-chain structure too large to properly mimic the tyrosine side-chain. Phenylalanine is nearly identical to
tyrosine, but lacks the primary alcohol used in catalysis. These facts make Answer A better than Answer choice C.
