## Passage 9 (Questions 49–52)

_Francisella novicida_ (_Fn_) is an avirulent bacteria found in normal human microflora. _Fn_ is closely related to a bacterial strain potentially lethal to humans, _Francisella tularensis_ (_Ftt_). _Fn_ and _Ftt_ differ in the expression of the Francisella Pathogenicity Island (FPI), found only in _Ftt_. FPI encodes for a type III secretion system that allows for dissemination of the bacteria from the infection site and broad-spectrum immunosuppression. Although _Fn_ is avirulent in humans, it can be lethal in rats.

Researchers conducted a study to determine if _Fn_ produces cross-reactive antibodies against _Ftt_.

**Experiment 1**

Rats were inoculated with _Fn_ that had been subjected to a virulence gene knockout (_iglD_⁻/⁻). The rats were then challenged thirty days later with either _Fn_ or _Ftt_. Table 1 shows the survival of five rats over a thirty-day post-challenge period.

**Table 1** — Survival of rats challenged with _Fn_ or _Ftt_

| Vaccination Strain | Challenge Strain | Survival (30 Days) |
|---|---|---|
| Mock | Fn | 0% |
| Fn iglD | Fn | 100% |
| Fn iglD | Ftt | 0% |

**Experiment 2**

In a second study, groups of rats were vaccinated with a vaccine comprised of either _Fn iglD_ knockout or _Ftt iglD_ knockout. A control group received a mock vaccine that did not contain an _iglD_ knockout. Thirty days after vaccination, serum was collected to assess antibody concentrations for the three vaccine groups. These results are displayed in Figure 1.

**Figure 1** — Antibody serum concentration in rats thirty days after vaccination

> _Bar graph: 50% Binding Titer (y-axis, log scale: 100–8000) for three antibody classes (Total Ab, IgG1, IgG2a). Three vaccine groups: Ftt iglD (dark gray), Fn iglD (light gray), Mock (black). Both Ftt iglD and Fn iglD show high titers (~7000) for Total Ab and IgG2a. All three groups show similarly low IgG1 levels (~100). Mock shows very low Total Ab and IgG2a (~100)._

Following the serum collection, the same rats were inoculated with a lethal dose of wild-type _Ftt_ and were monitored for mortality, as shown in Figure 2.

**Figure 2** — Mortality rate among rats inoculated with a lethal dose of wild-type _Ftt_ after previous vaccination with Mock, _Ftt iglD_ or _Fn iglD_

> _Line graph: % Survival (y-axis, 0–100%) vs. Days Post Challenge (x-axis, 0–30). Ftt iglD: remains near 100% survival throughout 30 days. Fn iglD: drops from 100% to ~20% survival by day 30. Mock: drops rapidly to ~0% by ~day 10._

_Adapted from P. Chu, Live Attenuated Francisella novicida Vaccine Protects against Francisella tularensis Pulmonary Challenge in Rats and Non-human Primates. PLOS Pathogens. © 2014._

### Question 50 ✓ (correct)

Researchers hypothesized that the _iglD_ gene encodes for proteins responsible for the immunosuppressive properties of _Ftt_. Which finding from the passage most strongly suggests that deletion of the _iglD_ gene increases host immune function?

- _Subtopic 402 — Antigen-antibody recognition · Topic 141 — Immune System · SIR 4: Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 2: Biology_

- **A. The increase in IgG2a and total antibody in Figure 1. ✓ (correct)**
- B. Survival of the rats challenged with _Fn_ in Table 1.
- C. Death of the rats vaccinated with _Fn iglD_ and _Ftt iglD_ in Figure 2.
- D. Lack of significant IgG1 production in Figure 1.

**Solution:** Answer choice A is the best choice. An increase in antibody production compared to the Mock control is evidence that knockout of the _iglD_ gene interferes with the bacteria's normal immunosuppressive effect, thus increasing host immune system function. Death of the rats in Figure 2, or a lack of IgG function, would indicate a weakened immune system, making answer choice C incorrect. Answer D is incorrect because lack of antibody production is not evidence of increased host immune system function. In any event, the data on IgG1 in Figure 1 is uninformative because the same very low antibody levels were observed for all three vaccines. Answer B should be rejected because survival of the rats challenged with _Fn_ in Table 1 could be the result of either the _Fn_ vaccination they received, or the fact that the _Fn_ component of that vaccine included an _iglD_ knockout. To isolate the impact of the knockout itself, we would need to see another trial that showed the results of an _Fn_ challenge after an _Fn_ vaccination that included the _iglD_ gene (_iglD_⁺/⁺).
