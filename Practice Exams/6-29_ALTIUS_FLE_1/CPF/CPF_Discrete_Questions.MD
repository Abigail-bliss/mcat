### Question 10 ✗ (missed)

The average height of an American adult is 1.70 m. When an individual of this height is standing, by how much will the blood pressure at the bottom of the feet differ from the blood pressure at the top of the head? (Density_blood = 1060 kg/m³)

- *Subtopic 515 — Hydrostatic pressure (Pascal's Law, Hydrostatic pressure, P = ρgh) · Topic 155 — Fluids · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 6: Physics*

- A. 2000 Pa — **✗ (her answer)**
- B. 6000 Pa
- C. 10000 Pa
- **D. 18000 Pa ✓ (correct)**

**Solution:** Pressure in a static fluid varies with depth: P = P_atm + ρgh. The pressure *difference* between feet and head is the ρgh term: ρgh = 1060 × 9.8 × 1.70 = 17,660 Pa ≈ **18,000 Pa (Choice D).** Approximating (1000 × 10 × 1.7 = 17,000 Pa) gets you to D just as fast. **Her trap (A, 2000 Pa):** likely a multiplication error / picking the wrong quantities. *Write P = ρgh, plug the three given numbers, estimate — don't eyeball.*

### Question 11 ✗ (missed)

Which change in solution composition would cause a protein to elute from a hydrophobic interaction column?

- *Subtopic 688 — Chromatography: Column chromatography, gas-liquid chromatography · Topic 178 — Separations and Purifications · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 5: Organic Chemistry*

- A. Decreasing pH
- B. Increasing pH
- **C. Decreasing salt concentration ✓ (correct)**
- D. Increasing salt concentration — **✗ (her answer)**

**Solution:** Hydrophobic interaction chromatography (HIC) relies on **high** salt to strengthen hydrophobic interactions between the protein and the column. **Decreasing** salt weakens those interactions, so the protein dissociates and elutes (Choice C). pH has no significant role in HIC (A and B out; charge neutralization is a small secondary effect at most). **Her trap (D):** increasing salt would *strengthen* binding — the opposite of eluting. *Key fact: HIC = "salt on to bind, salt off to elute" (the reverse of ion-exchange in feel).*

### Question 12

Students added HCl dropwise to a solution of phospholipids with an initial pH of 7.4. Upon reaching a pH of 4.0, what changes will have occurred to the [COO⁻] and [COOH] present on the phospholipid head?

- *Subtopic 652 — Weak acids and bases: dissociation of weak acids and bases with or without added salt · Topic 172 — Acid; Base Equilibria · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 4: General Chemistry*

- A. Increased [COO⁻], because water will abstract more protons.
- **B. Increased [COOH], because water will donate more protons. ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- C. Increased [COO⁻], because the [COOH] increased.
- D. Increased [COOH], because the [COO⁻] increased.

**Solution:** Apply Le Chatelier to RCOOH + H₂O → RCOO⁻ + H₃O⁺. Lowering the pH means [H₃O⁺] rises, shifting the dissociation equilibrium **left** (toward reactants), increasing the protonated carboxylic acid [COOH]. That proton can come from water or hydronium. A and C are wrong because [COO⁻] *decreases*, not increases. D is wrong because the acid and base cannot both increase simultaneously (and it makes the same reversed claim as C).
