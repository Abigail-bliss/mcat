## 2026-06-28 — Strategy pivot: review > intake

**Diagnosis:** Time is going ~100% to **intake** (lectures, doing questions, logging) and ~0%
to **consolidation** (reviewing misses until they stick). Intake feels like progress but doesn't
move scores. This is the same trap as before (card-*making*) wearing a new costume (a miss log
she never reviews). A log you don't revisit is the same waste as cards you don't review.

**Decisions:**
1. **Logging = reviewing, collapsed into one act.** At the moment of a miss: understand *why*,
   re-derive the right answer, say the concept aloud (that's the encoding), then log **one line**.
   No separate circle-back. If forced to choose, **review wins** — the log serves the review.
2. **Triage lectures by strength.** Keep full lectures only for Bio/Biochem/Orgo (1.25–1.5×, quiz
   immediately). Skim/skip Gen Chem, Physics-review, Psych/Soc — learn those through questions +
   explanation review. The ADHD lecture tax is the biggest, lowest-leverage time sink.
3. **FLs:** Altius is effectively unlimited, so don't hoard — but the bottleneck is *review time*,
   not FL supply. Sustainable cadence now = ~1 FL / 1–2 weeks, each with a review day. Ramp to
   ~2/week in PTO. Save AAMC FLs for the final ~3 weeks; end on AAMC.
4. **Anki with caution = don't *make* cards; do *review* premade.** Hold off adding AnKing load
   until the question-review habit is stable; it's the spaced layer on top, ramped in PTO.

**Reframe of the 10 weeks (5 working + 5 PTO):** working weeks = finish first-pass *exposure* +
switch on the review loop (not mastery). PTO = the real engine where 496 climbs.
