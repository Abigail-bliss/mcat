### Question 23 ✓ (correct)

What is the most likely result of a mutation in chymotrypsin from Asp-102 to Glu-102?

- *Subtopic 705 — Classification (acidic or basics, hydrophilic or hydrophobic) · Topic 180 — Amino Acids, Peptides, Proteins · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 1: Biochemistry*

- A. Total loss of protein catabolism
- B. Large increase in protein catabolism
- **C. Small decrease in protein catabolism ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- D. No change to protein catabolism

**Solution:** The mutation from Asp to Glu is a *complementary* (conservative) substitution — the only difference between Asp and Glu is a single additional carbon between the alpha carbon and the carboxylic acid functional group. This minor change, with no change to the functional group itself, is unlikely to significantly impact chemistry, so activity is likely retained. The increased size may distort the active site *slightly*, but activity is largely preserved → **small decrease (Choice C)**. Answer A is wrong (enzyme remains active). Answer B is wrong (no reason a conservative substitution would dramatically increase catabolism). Answer D is wrong because any physical change to the enzyme is likely to have at least *some* effect (enzyme active sites are tight, geometry-specific spaces). *Test-taking note: all three distractors use definitive language ("Total," "Large," "No"); Choice C is the only option that allows for nuance — watch for over-stated logic to eliminate wrong choices.*

### Question 24 ✓ (correct)

The S_z solution most likely contains which protease?

- *Topic 21 — Enzyme Structure and Function · SIR 4: Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 1: Biochemistry*

- A. Trypsinogen
- B. Chymotrypsin
- **C. Pepsin ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- D. Gastric amylase

**Solution:** Figure 3 shows two enzymes — pepsin and gastric lipase — with a pH optimum near 2.5. Per Table 1, enzyme activity in the S_z solution is maximal at pH 2.5, so the unknown is most likely gastric lipase or pepsin. But the passage and stem both ask for a *protease*, which gastric lipase is not (lipase acts on fats). Therefore the unknown must be **pepsin (Choice C)**. Answer B is wrong because chymotrypsin's optimum pH is much higher (Figure 3). Answer A: trypsinogen is closely related to chymotrypsin and is secreted into the duodenum (~pH 6.0), ruling it out. Answer D is wrong because amylase is not a protease. *Match the pH-optimum data to Figure 3, then filter by the "protease" constraint in the stem.*
