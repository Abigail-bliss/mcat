## Passage 8 (Questions 39–42)

Human norovirus is the source of over 90% of non-bacterial gastroenteritis cases worldwide. The non-structural norovirus protein NS1-2 has been the subject of extensive research. The viral gene ORF1 codes for a polyprotein that is cleaved into six mature peptides (NS1-2, and NS3 through NS7). NS1-2 prevents protein secretion in infected cells. Researchers conducted multiple experiments to better understand NS1-2.

The sequence of the murine norovirus NS1-2 protein was analyzed to predict which regions might be in a disordered state (lacking fixed three-dimensional structure). Disordered protein segments are of interest when characterizing proteins because they are the segments most likely to be involved in protein-protein binding interactions. Disordered regions are also related to protein solubility. Disordered regions are more likely to be found on the periphery of a protein and tend to increase its solubility, whereas highly-ordered regions are more often found inside of a protein's hydrophobic core.

**Figure 1** — Disorder predictions (DP) for the full NS1-2 protein; DP values greater than 0.5 are predicted to be disordered. *Disorder Prediction (0–1.0) vs. R{ esidue Number  | whats a residue number? }(0–350). DP is high (>0.5) at the N-terminal region (roughly residues 1–142), then mostly low (ordered) through the middle, with some fluctuations.*

Four variants of the murine NS1-2 protein were constructed for in vitro characterization experiments: 1) the full-length NS1-2 protein (NS1-2full), 2) the truncated NS1-2 dimer (NS1-2trunc), 3) the caspase-cleaved NS1-2 monomer (NS1-2casp), and 4) the C-terminus-deleted, truncated, ordered region NS1-2 monomer (NS1-2ord). The amino acid sequences for each variant are shown in Figure 2.

**Figure 2** — Schematic of the recombinant viral protein expression constructs for NS1-2. *ORF1 polyprotein: NS1-2 · NS3 · NS4 · NS5 · NS6 · NS7. Constructs: NS1-2full (37.6 kDa) a.a. 1–350, with Disordered region a.a. 1–142; NS1-2trunc (29.4 kDa) a.a. 7–260; NS1-2casp (13.7 kDa) a.a. 7–121; NS1-2ord (14.1 kDa) a.a. 143–260.*

The Stokes radii (R_s) is the radius of a hard sphere that diffuses at the same rate as an analyte and is therefore predicted to have the same radius. ## Questions

### Question 39 ✓ (correct)

What percentage of NS1-2 amino acids are disordered based on the disorder predictions in Figure 1?

- *Subtopic 75 — Conformational stability (denaturing, folding, hydrophobic interactions, solvation layer; entropy) · Topic 19 — Protein Structures · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 1: Biochemistry*

- **A. 39% ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- B. 49%
- C. 60%
- D. 97%

**Solution:** 39% of the NS1-2 protein is disordered. Per the Figure 1 legend, DP > 0.5 = disordered, so the {N-terminal region} is predicted disordered (DP > 0.5). Figure 2 also labels a region of NS1-2full as "Disordered, a.a. 1–142." The x-axis of Figure 1 = number of amino acids; use disordered (142) over total (350): 142/350 = 40.5% ≈ **39% (Choice A)**. (A small ordered stretch in the disordered section and a small disordered stretch in the ordered section roughly cancel.) Answer B (49%) ≈ if there were ~200 disordered a.a. Answer C could come from miscounting the DP < 0.5 residues. Answer D is wrong: Figure 1 shows nowhere near 100% is disordered. *Choices are deliberately close (30/35/40/45 style) — this is a "no-calculator" estimation trap; compute 142/350 rather than eyeball.*

### Question 40 ✓ (correct)

The structural state wherein a globular protein is partially extended is characterized as:

- *Subtopic 75 — Conformational stability (denaturing, folding, hydrophobic interactions, solvation layer; entropy) · Topic 19 — Protein Structures · SIR 1: Knowledge of Scientific Concepts and Principles · Discipline 1: Biochemistry*

- A. unfolded.
- **B. premolten globule. ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- C. denatured.
- D. natively folded.

**Solution:** A protein in a premolten globule state is beginning to fold — more folded/ordered than an unfolded protein, but less ordered than the molten globule state. The molten globule is almost completely folded and is often said to have "native-like" properties. A completely folded protein is a "native protein" (Answer D). The relative degree of folding of each model is revealed by the Stokes radii in Table 2, which order by increasing size/radius as: Natively Folded < Molten Globule < Premolten Globule < Unfolded → **Choice B**. Answer A is wrong (unfolded ≠ partially extended, which is more folded). Answer C is wrong: a completely denatured protein wouldn't fit "globular protein that is partially extended." Answer D is wrong: a native protein is fully collapsed with well-formed 3-D structure. *Molten vs. premolten globule sits at the edge of first-semester biochem — the three distractors don't describe "partially extended," so it's answerable even without recalling the exact definition.*

### Question 41 ✓ (correct)

Norovirus is a single-strand RNA virus. Norovirus nucleic acids do NOT contain:

- *Subtopic 87 — Nucleotides and nucleosides (sugar phosphate backbone, pyrimidine, purine residues) · Topic 23 — Nucleic Acid Structure and Function · SIR 1: Knowledge of Scientific Concepts and Principles · Discipline 2: Biology*

- A. Ribose
- B. Adenine
- **C. Deoxyribose ✓ (correct — her answer)**
- D. Uracil

**Solution:** RNA does NOT contain deoxyribose, which is found in DNA → **Choice C**. Deoxyribose has a hydrogen attached to the 2′ carbon, whereas ribose has a hydroxyl group in that same position. Answer A is false because RNA contains ribose. Answers B and D are false because RNA contains both adenine and uracil (as well as guanine and cytosine). RNA does not contain thymine, which is replaced by uracil.

### Question 42 ✗ (missed)

Experimental data suggest NS1-2 exists as a dimer. Which size-exclusion chromatography spectra is consistent with an NS1-2 mixture which may contain a folded monomer, an unfolded monomer, or a folded dimer?

- *Subtopic 686 — Extraction: distribution of solute between two immiscible solvents · Topic 178 — Separations and Purifications · SIR 4: Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 5: Organic Chemistry*

- A. SEC spectrum labeled: Peak 1 = folded monomer, Peak 2 = dimer
- **B. SEC spectrum labeled: Peak 1 = dimer, Peak 2 = folded monomer ✓ (correct)**
- C. SEC spectrum labeled: Peak 1 = folded monomer, Peak 2 = unfolded monomer — **✗ (her answer)**
- D. SEC spectrum labeled: Peak 1 = unfolded monomer, Peak 2 = dimer

**Solution:** In size-exclusion chromatography larger molecules migrate *faster* and smaller molecules migrate more slowly (smaller molecules spend more time in the tiny pores of the stationary phase; larger molecules can't enter the pores and pass straight through to the bottom). A dimer is larger than a monomer, so it elutes first (Peak 1 = dimer), followed by the folded monomer (Peak 2) → **Choice B**. Answer A is backwards (dimer would migrate faster than the folded monomer, not slower). Answer C is wrong: an unfolded monomer has a larger radius and would migrate *faster* than the folded monomer. Answer D is wrong: the dimer again migrates faster than a monomer — either folded or unfolded. *(Per Table 2, unfolded proteins have much larger radii than folded proteins.)* *This tests order-of-elution (by size) AND applying it to a visual spectrum.*
