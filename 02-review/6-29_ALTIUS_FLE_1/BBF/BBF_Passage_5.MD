## Passage 5 (Questions 23–27)

The barrier function of the skin protects the mammalian body against infection, dehydration, UV irradiation, and
temperature fluctuation. Barrier function is reduced during the normal aging process. Evidence suggests this decline
results in part from the failure of tight junctions (TJs). TJs are essential not only for dividing epidermal cells into
apical and basolateral compartments to create cell polarity, but also for the skin's barrier function. TJs enhance the
barrier function by regulating the selective permeability of the paracellular pathway. The selectivity of TJs is imparted
by Claudins (Cldns), { a family of 23 highly conserved tetraspan membrane proteins | idk what this means... highly conserved? tetraspan? } whose function stems from charged amino
acid sequences on the {cytoplasmic C-terminus tail}.

{Transgenic} mice with a Cldn6 deficiency in the {suprabasal layers of the epidermis} undergo a {perturbed terminal
differentiation program} characterized by reduced barrier function. To examine the mechanisms by which Cldn6 acts during
epithelial differentiation, researchers overexpressed a Cldn6 cytoplasmic tail deletion mutant (Inv-Cldn6-CΔ196) in the
suprabasal (upper) compartment of the transgenic mouse epidermis. No gross phenotypic abnormalities were observed at
birth, but with aging, chronic dermatitis appeared with a {concomitant} barrier dysfunction manifested as increased
trans-epidermal water loss.

Genotyping was performed by PCR on mutant and wild-type (WT) mice using {genomic DNA and primers} specific for Cldn6
(5'-ATGGCCTCTACTGGTCTGCA-3'). Age-matched WT and Inv-Cldn6-CΔ196 transgenic mice were photographed and histological
slides of skin were taken from the neck, the abdomen, and behind the ears. Immunohistochemical analysis revealed {aberrant}
Cldn localization to the {cytosolic leaflet of the epithelial membrane} and only one {transmembrane Cldn6 moiety}.

_Adapted from: Troy T-C, Arabzadeh A, Larivière NMK, Enikanolaiye A, Turksen K (2009) Dermatitis and Aging-Related
Barrier Dysfunction in Transgenic Mice Overexpressing an Epidermal-Targeted Claudin 6 Tail Deletion Mutant. PLoS ONE
4(11): e7814. doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0007814_

## Questions

### Question 23 ✓ (correct)

If a segment of DNA complementary to the Cldn6 primer was transcribed into mRNA, would a start codon be found in the
transcript?

- _Subtopic 1182 — General · Topic 26 — Genetic Code · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem Solving · Discipline 2:
  Biology_

- A. Yes, because there would be an ATG sequence.
- B. No, because there would be no ATG sequence.
- **C. Yes, because there would be an AUG sequence. ✓ (correct)**
- D. No, because there would be no AUG sequence.

**Solution:** Choice C is correct. The primer ATG GCC TCT ACT GGT CTG CA would result in a complimentary sequence of DNA
reading TAC CGG AGA TGA CCA GAC GT, which would transcribe back into mRNA with the sequence: AUG GCC UCU ACU GGU CUG CA.
The first three bases are the start codon AUG, so yes, there is a start codon in the transcript. This makes answer choice
C correct and eliminates answer choice D. Answers A and B can be eliminated immediately because the thymine base (T) is
not found in RNA.

### Question 24 ✗ (missed)

The {enterotoxin} of the bacterium _Clostridium perfringens_ {upregulates} the expression of human claudins, including Cldn6.
Given the information in the passage, how does _C. perfringens_ enterotoxin impact epithelial barrier function?

- _Subtopic 482 — Structure: layer differentiation, cell types · Topic 148 — Skin System · SIR 4: Data-Based and
  Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 2: Biology_

- A. Epithelial cells exhibit reversed polarity.
- **B. Epithelial barrier function is enhanced. ✓ (correct)**
- C. Epithelial barrier function declines. _(your answer)_
- D. Enterotoxin does not impact barrier function.

**Solution:** Answer B is correct. The passage states that the selectivity of TJs enhances barrier function, and that TJ
selectivity is imparted by Claudins. Therefore, upregulation of Claudins is expected to enhance the barrier function.
This makes Answer B correct. Answer C is false because, based solely on passage information, increased Cldn6 should
enhance TJ selectivity and overall barrier function. Answer A is false because TJs are essential for establishing cell
polarity (Paragraph 1). Answer D should be eliminated because the stem clearly states that the enterotoxin impacts
Claudin expression, so it is expected to also impact the barrier function. Answer D illustrates the foolishness of one
common "strategy" suggested by many status quo MCAT prep companies: namely, that answer choices like "none of the above"
or "does not have an effect" are more likely to be the correct answer. Our algorithmic analysis of all AAMC questions
shows no data in support of such a conclusion. You should not trust your MCAT score on some mindless "gimmick." Your best
chance of answering correctly is to understand the passage and question clearly and make your choice based on science
knowledge and sound logic.

### Question 25 ✓ (correct)

A disruption in the expression of Cldn6 is LEAST likely to affect which layer of the epidermis?

- _Subtopic 482 — Structure: layer differentiation, cell types · Topic 148 — Skin System · SIR 1: Knowledge of Scientific
  Concepts and Principles · Discipline 2: Biology_

> _Figure: cross-section of the epidermis labeled top-to-bottom — Stratum corneum, Stratum lucidum, Stratum granulosum,
> Stratum spinosum, Stratum basale — sitting above the Dermis._

- A. Stratum corneum
- B. Stratum granulosum
- C. Stratum spinosum
- **D. Stratum basale ✓ (correct)**

**Solution:** Choice D is correct. According to the passage, the effects of altered Cldn6 expression are observed in the
suprabasal layers of the epithelium, which are the uppermost layers, above the stratum basale. The suprabasal layers
consist of the stratum corneum (answer A), the stratum granulosum (B), and the stratum spinosum (C). However, specific
memorized knowledge is not necessary to answer the question. The term "suprabasal" used in the passage suggests layers
_above_ the basal layer, and the figure provided in the stem makes it clear how each layer is ordered. Answer choice D,
the stratum basale, as its name implies, is the basal layer of the skin and therefore least likely to be affected by a
condition that impacts suprabasal epithelium.

### Question 26 ✓ (correct)

Abnormal expression of Cldn6 has been implicated in the spread of cancerous melanoma cells via an increased blood supply
to the surface of the skin. If it is demonstrated that decreases in normal barrier function are correlated with increased
blood supply to the epidermis, how might Cldn6 be contributing to this effect?

- _Subtopic 490 — Hormonal control: sweating, vasodilation, and vasoconstriction · Topic 148 — Skin System · SIR 4:
  Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning · Discipline 2: Biology_

- **A. Abnormal expression of Cldn6 increases blood supply to melanoma cells. ✓ (correct)**
- B. Normal expression of Cldn6 increases blood supply to melanoma cells.
- C. Decreased expression of Cldn6 decreases blood supply to melanoma cells.
- D. Overexpression of Cldn6 decreases blood supply to melanoma cells.

**Solution:** Choice A is correct. The question stem states that there is a correlation between decreased barrier function
and increased blood supply, and that blood supply is implicated in the spread of melanoma. Therefore, if Cldn6 is
contributing to this effect it is most likely doing so through some mechanism that increases blood supply to the
epidermis. This makes Answer A correct. It also eliminates Answers C and D because decreased blood supply should limit
the spread of melanoma. Answer B is false because normal expression of Cldn6 should not contribute to the spread of
melanoma; the stem stated that "abnormal" expression has been implicated in the spread of cancerous melanoma cells.

### Question 27 ✓ (correct)

Sweat pores in the skin release water and various other substances onto the surface of the skin. What physical property
of water allows sweating to reduce body temperature?

- _Subtopic 1111 — General · Topic 177 — Liquid Phase–Intermolecular Forces · SIR 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem
  Solving · Discipline 5: Organic Chemistry_

- A. Lower density in the solid phase
- **B. High heat capacity ✓ (correct)**
- C. Low heat capacity
- D. High surface tension

**Solution:** Answer choice B is correct. Water has a number of properties that make it uniquely suited to serve as "the
solvent of life." Most of these are due to the strong intermolecular forces among water molecules and its polarity. The
one that is most relevant to cooling the body is water's high heat capacity — when water molecules acquire enough energy
to evaporate from the surface of the skin, they have absorbed a relatively large amount of heat and therefore take away a
large amount of heat. This is known as evaporative cooling. Answers A and D describe properties of water, but are not
relevant to evaporative cooling. Answer C is incorrect as water has a high, not a low, heat capacity.

## Terms & Notes

### Q24 — enterotoxin / barrier function (the causal-chain miss)

**Enterotoxin** — A bacterial toxin that targets the **gut/epithelium** ("entero-" = intestine). *C. perfringens* enterotoxin happens to **bind and upregulate claudins**. For this question, ignore the scary connotation — the stem tells you exactly what it does: it **upregulates claudins**.

**Upregulates** — **Increases** the expression/amount of something (here, more claudin protein). Opposite = downregulate.

**The reasoning (build the chain with arrows):** the passage gives ↑Claudin → ↑TJ selectivity → ↑barrier function. The stem adds: enterotoxin → ↑claudins. Chain it:

> **enterotoxin → Cldn ↑ → TJ selectivity ↑ → barrier function ↑ = ENHANCED (B).**

**Why C ("declines") was the trap — two pulls:**
1. **"Enterotoxin" connotes harm** → *toxin = bad = barrier breaks*. But answer from "upregulates claudins," not from the word's vibe.
2. **The passage experiment ran the chain the *other* way** — it was a Cldn6 *deletion/loss-of-function* → *reduced* barrier. Easy to pattern-match "Claudin + barrier = dysfunction" and miss that this stem goes **UP**. Direction is the whole question.

*The fix (your own diagnosis):* for any "how does X affect Y" question, **write the causal chain on scratch paper with ↑/↓ arrows** before choosing. It externalizes the logic so connotation and pattern-matching can't hijack you.
