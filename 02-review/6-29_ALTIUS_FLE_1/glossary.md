## Nucleic acids & central dogma

**Sense vs. anti-sense strand** — **Sense (coding)** strand = same sequence as mRNA (T→U). **Anti-sense (template)** strand = complementary to mRNA; the strand RNA pol reads. An oligo complementary to the **sense** strand is complementary to the **mRNA** → binds & blocks it. *(Cost Q7 + Q10.)*

**Start & stop codons** — Start = **AUG** (Met, begins translation). Stop = **UAA, UAG, UGA** (no amino acid). Template DNA **TAC** → mRNA **AUG**. *(Q23 — reasoned right, real gap.)*

**Oligonucleotides** — Short (a few–~50 bases) usually single-stranded DNA/RNA made to base-pair with a target. Tool behind **antisense knockdown**.

**`kb` vs `kDa`** — **kb** = kilobase = 1,000 nucleotide bases (nucleic-acid *length*). **kDa** = kilodalton (protein *mass*). Don't swap them.

## Molecular & genetics techniques

**Mouse knock-out studies** — Inactivate one gene, observe what breaks → reveals the gene's normal job. Loss-of-function logic.

**Chromatography — match method to property** *(Orgo, weakness)*:
- **Size-exclusion (gel-filtration)** → separates by **size**; large elutes **first** (small gets trapped in pores).
- **Ion-exchange** → by **charge**; **anion-exchange** resin is +, binds − proteins; **cation-exchange** resin is −, binds + proteins (net charge set by pH vs pI).
- **Metal-affinity** → by **specific binding**; Ni²⁺ binds a **His-tag**, one-step purification.

**Affinity tag** — An engineered handle (commonly a **His-tag**) so a column can grab the protein specifically (His-tag ↔ Ni²⁺). Affinity = a *specific binding partner*, not a bulk property.

## Chromatin & histones

**Histones (normal function + location)** — Proteins that DNA wraps around to condense; they **bind/stabilize DNA** (not RNA) and sit in the **nucleus** (= a neuron's **soma**). *(Cost Q21 + Q22.)*

**"Neurotoxic factors = histones" (reading lesson)** — Para 2 said the toxins were *identified* and used to make the conditioned medium; Para 3 said histones H1/H2B were *in* that medium ⇒ the toxins **are** the histones. Trap: "**representative**" describes the medium (a rebuilt stand-in), not "fake without the toxin." H1's toxicity was stated **3×** (para link, Figure 1, last paragraph).

## Neuroscience (neurons & synapses)

**Dendritic spines** — Small protrusions on dendrites; the **postsynaptic** site of most excitatory synapses. Number/shape ↔ synaptic strength; the structural basis of **neuroplasticity / learning**.

**Neuromuscular junction (NMJ)** — Synapse between a **motor neuron** and a **skeletal muscle fiber**: ACh → nicotinic receptors → depolarization → contraction. Agrin organizes the ACh receptors.

**Axon extension** ("extended axons vigorously") — Active **axon outgrowth** (via growth cones) = a marker of **healthy/growing** neurons.

## Cell biology & organelles

**Mitochondrial anatomy** — Two membranes → three compartments. **Cristae** = folds of the inner membrane (not a compartment). **Matrix** = innermost space (TCA cycle, mtDNA). **Intermembrane space** = H⁺ reservoir pumped by the ETC.

**ATP synthase** — **F₀** spans the inner membrane (proton channel); **F₁** (α/β head) projects into the **matrix** (makes ATP). Protons flow F₀ → drives F₁.

**Enzyme-suffix shortcuts** — **kinase** = adds phosphate from ATP; **phosphatase** = removes it; **synthase** = joins (no ATP) vs **synthetase/ligase** = joins (uses ATP); **dehydrogenase** = redox. *(ATP synthase is special — driven by the proton gradient, not a normal condensation.)*

**Moiety** — A distinct **part/portion of a molecule** (e.g., "the proton-channel moiety" = F₀).

## Protein structure & biochemistry

**Protein domains (LNS, KSPI, EGF)** — A **domain** = an independently-folding *module* with its own job; proteins combine/repeat several ("multidomain"). An **inhibitor domain** *blocks* an enzyme (KSPI inhibits serine proteases). Don't memorize the names — know the modular-structure concept.

## Immunology

**Innate vs. adaptive immunity** — Innate = **non-specific** (inflammation; fast; no memory). Adaptive = **specific** (two kinds: **cell-mediated** + **humoral**; has memory). Trap: "**non-specific adaptive immunity**" does not exist. *(Cost Q19; Immune System = weakest BB topic.)*

## Pathology & tissue response

**Hypertrophy vs. hyperplasia** — Hypertrophy = bigger **existing** cells; hyperplasia = **more** cells. (Reactive gliosis = both.)

**Ischemia** — **Inadequate blood supply** → O₂/glucose deprivation → cell injury/death (classic: **stroke**).

## Epithelium / skin barrier

**Enterotoxin** — A bacterial toxin targeting the gut/epithelium; *C. perfringens* enterotoxin **upregulates claudins**. Don't let "toxin" connote harm — read what it *does*.

**Upregulate / downregulate** — Increase / decrease the expression (amount) of something.

## General vocabulary

**Morphology** — Form/structure (vs. function); e.g., dendrite shape, length, spine count.
